# Theme to curate against

theme: >
  Human bulk, single-cell, single-nucleus, or spatial transcriptomic datasets
  containing at least one profiled sample with established fibrosis or an
  explicitly documented fibrotic phenotype.

objective: >
  Curate gene-expression datasets suitable for investigating shared and
  organ- or disease-specific transcriptional patterns of fibrosis across human
  tissues, diseases, cell populations, and spatial regions.

dataset_level_inclusion:
  - The publication or metadata must reference a dataset containing gene-expression measurements.
  - The assay must be bulk transcriptomics, single-cell transcriptomics,
    single-nucleus transcriptomics, or spatial transcriptomics.
  - At least one profiled biological sample, specimen, tissue region, cell
    population, culture, organoid, or ex vivo preparation must satisfy the
    fibrosis evidence criteria below.
  - A dataset qualifies as a whole when it contains at least one qualifying
    fibrotic sample. Non-fibrotic controls and comparator samples in the same
    dataset should be retained.
  - Samples must be human-derived. Animal-only datasets do not qualify.

relevant_direct_evidence:
  - The profiled samples are explicitly described as fibrotic, fibrosed,
    scarred, or originating from fibrosis patients or fibrotic lesions.
  - The profiled samples have a clinical or pathological diagnosis of fibrosis,
    including a fibrosis stage, histological fibrosis assessment, or documented
    fibrotic remodelling.
  - The profiled samples come from a condition whose defining sampled state
    establishes advanced fibrosis, such as explicitly diagnosed cirrhotic liver
    tissue.
  - The publication or dataset metadata explicitly links the profiled samples
    to organ fibrosis, disease-associated fibrosis, or a fibrotic tissue state.
  - Spatial regions or transcriptomic cell populations are explicitly annotated
    as belonging to fibrotic tissue, fibrotic lesions, scars, or fibrotic niches.

unsure_evidence:
  - Human samples are exposed to a fibrosis-inducing or profibrotic treatment,
    but an established fibrotic phenotype is not demonstrated.
  - The samples show fibroblast activation, myofibroblast differentiation,
    extracellular-matrix production, collagen expression, tissue stiffening,
    wound healing, or tissue remodelling without explicit evidence of fibrosis.
  - The samples come from a disease frequently associated with fibrosis, but
    fibrosis is not confirmed for the profiled cohort or specimens.
  - Fibrosis is described for the broader study population, but it is unclear
    whether the sequenced samples are the fibrotic samples.
  - The publication appears relevant, but the available text or metadata does
    not establish the assay type or the connection between fibrosis and the
    profiled samples.
  - The dataset contains induced human in-vitro or ex-vivo fibrosis models
    without direct confirmation that fibrosis was achieved.

not_sufficient_alone:
  - Expression of individual profibrotic markers.
  - Presence of fibroblasts or myofibroblasts.
  - Collagen, extracellular-matrix, TGF-beta, mechanotransduction, wound-healing,
    or tissue-remodelling terminology.
  - A disease name that can be associated with fibrosis.
  - Fibrosis mentioned only in the introduction, discussion, cited literature,
    future directions, or general biological background.
  - A profibrotic perturbation applied to otherwise non-fibrotic samples.
  - Fibrosis measured only as an outcome outside the samples subjected to
    transcriptomic profiling.

exclude:
  - Animal-only datasets.
  - Studies without gene-expression or transcriptomic measurements.
  - Proteomics-only, metabolomics-only, epigenomics-only, imaging-only,
    histology-only, or genotype-only datasets.
  - Studies of healthy fibroblast biology with no fibrosis-linked sample state.
  - Cancer-associated fibroblast or tumour-stroma datasets unless the profiled
    samples are explicitly linked to fibrosis or a fibrotic tissue state.
  - Acute wound-healing studies without established or plausibly persistent
    fibrotic remodelling.
  - Publications that discuss fibrosis but do not connect it to samples in the
    referenced dataset.

evidence_requirements:
  - Extract evidence about both the assay and the fibrosis status of the
    actually profiled samples.
  - Do not infer sample-level fibrosis from general background statements.
  - Do not transfer a fibrosis label from one cohort or experiment to a
    different set of profiled samples.
  - Prefer sample annotations, cohort descriptions, pathology, clinical
    diagnoses, fibrosis stages, experimental-group definitions, and dataset
    metadata.
  - When fibrosis is inferred rather than explicit, explain the biological or
    clinical link and return an unsure judgement.

judgement_rules:
  relevant: >
    The evidence establishes that the dataset uses an allowed human
    transcriptomic assay and contains at least one profiled sample with
    established or explicitly documented fibrosis.
  unsure: >
    The study plausibly contains relevant human transcriptomic samples, but the
    fibrosis phenotype, assay eligibility, or connection to the profiled
    samples is indirect or incomplete. Induced fibrosis without confirmation
    belongs here.
  not_relevant: >
    The dataset lacks an allowed transcriptomic assay, lacks qualifying human
    fibrotic samples, or mentions fibrosis only as background or as biology not
    demonstrated in the profiled samples.
